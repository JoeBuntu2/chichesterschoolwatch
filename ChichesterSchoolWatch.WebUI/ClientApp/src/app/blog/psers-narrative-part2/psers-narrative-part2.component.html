Bartholf Uses PSERS As Scapegoat for $2 Million Deficit

Last year, Chichester School District's preliminary budget featured a $2 million deficit (which ultimately grew into a $3.47 million deficit in the final budget). At the 5-22-19 Chichester School Board Meeting, I asked the school board the following question about the preliminary budget deficit:
How do we plan to resolve the 2 million dollar deficit?

Region 1 board member David Bartholf seized the opportunity to push his PSERS narrative. In a convincing speech, he went to great lengths to suggest that increasing pension costs (PSERS) were responsible for the deficit. I have to admit, he did a great job at selling his narrative. With his respectable roles as Board Treasurer and Finance Committee member, combined with his authoritative delivery, who wouldn't believe him?

Have a look at the video below. What you should know is that pensions (PSERS) had absolutely nothing to do with the deficit. In fact, Chichester's share of pensions (PSERS) decreased for the second year in a row. I cover the actual impact of PSERS on Chichester SD finances extensively and show you how to verify the data yourself in part 1 of this series - Part 1: The Actual Impact of PSERS on Chichester SD.

Video Analysis: Bartholf's Narrative vs Reality

Let's discuss the above video. Behind every narrative are some bits of truth. While Mr Bartholf doesn't exactly lie, he does conveniently leave out critical information. While he doesn't explicitly state: 'PSERS caused the $2 million deficit in the preliminary budget', he blindfolds and leads taxpayers to the edge of the cliff he has crafted.

Consider The Following:
  1. Remember the question asked is 'How does the board plan to resolve the 2 million dollar deficit?'. Mr Bartholf goes on and on talking about PSERS, painting a distorted portrait of district finances. He neglects to mention how Chichester's share of PSERS has decreased two years in a row and has absolutely nothing to do with the deficit. Check out the chart below or read more here about the actual numbers.: Part 1: The Actual Impact of PSERS on Chichester SD.
  2. He places great emphasis on contribution rates, how they are currently around '33%', and how they increase every year. He goes on to assert that their is nothing built into the budget to handle these increases. While the rates are high, as discussed in Part 1, Chichester isn't actually on the hook for that entire amount. He neglects to mention the existence of state reimbursement money (specifically for PSERS), how state money they receive covers more than half the share of pension contributions, or how it increases every year as well. He leaves out the part that state money has increased so much, that Chichester's share has decreased 2 years in a row. (see chart below)
  3. He boasts about the boards '10-Year Plan' where a bunch of taxpayer money has been stashed away to supposedly mitigate future rate increases, even suggesting that the board is ahead of the pack. This is all just setup for when the ultimate truth is finally realized at the end of the video - they planned to use the PSERS fund to cover the 2 million dollar deficit. He neglects to mention how they planned once-again to funnel money through a fund named after PSERS to cover a deficit having nothing to do with PSERS. This time they planned to funnel 2 million dollars through said fund.

...ultimately the board managed to grow the $2 million preliminary budget deficit into a $3.47 million deficit in the final budget. The majority of that deficit, having nothing to do with PSERS, was of course covered by the PSERS fund.
