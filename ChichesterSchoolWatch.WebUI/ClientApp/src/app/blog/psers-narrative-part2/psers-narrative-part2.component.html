Bartholf Misleads Taxpayers at 5-22-19 Board Meeting

Last year, Chichester School District's preliminary budget featured a $2 million deficit (which ultimately grew into a $3.47 million deficit in the final budget). At the 5-22-19 Chichester School Board Meeting, I asked the school board the following question about the preliminary budget deficit:
How do we plan to resolve the 2 million dollar deficit?

Region 1 board member David Bartholf responded, seizing the opportunity to push the PSERS narrative. In a convincing speech, he went to great lengths to suggest that increasing pension costs (PSERS) were responsible for the deficit. I have to admit, he did a great job at selling his narrative. With his respectable roles as Board Treasurer and Finance Committee member, combined with his authoritative delivery, who wouldn't believe him?

Have a look at the video below. What you should know is that pensions (PSERS) had absolutely nothing to do with the deficit. In fact, Chichester's share of pensions (PSERS) decreased for the second year in a row. I cover the actual impact of PSERS on Chichester SD finances extensively and show you how to verify the data yourself in part 1 of this series - Part 1: The Actual Impact of PSERS on Chichester SD.

Bartholf's Narrative vs Reality
Contender #1: Bartholf's Narrative
Bartholf has to build his case for why money would be used from a PSERS fund to cover the deficit. Here are his talking points.
  • How the PSERS contribution rate goes up every year.
  • How the rate is currently around 33%.
  • How they don't have anything built in to the budget to handle rate increases.
  • How the board has planned for these increases with a genius 10-year plan where a bunch of tax-payer money has been stashed away in a PSERS fund to help ease the burden of future PSERS rate increases.
Contender #2: Reality
Bartholf conveniently leaves out some important details. Most importantly, he forgets to mention that PSERS has nothing to do with the deficit.
  • Remember the question asked is 'How does the board plan to resolve the 2 million dollar deficit?'. He neglects to mention how Chichester's share of PSERS has decreased two years in a row and has absolutely nothing to do with the deficit. See here: Part 1: The Actual Impact of PSERS on Chichester SD.
  • He mentions increasing contribution rates, but he neglects to mention anything about state reimbursement money for PSERS, the fact that this state money covers more than half the pensions cost, and have also been increasing every year. (see graph below).
  • He boasts about the boards '10-Year Plan', but neglects to mention how they planned once-again to funnel money through a fund named after PSERS to cover a deficit having nothing to do with PSERS. This time they planned to funnel 2 million dollars through said fund.
