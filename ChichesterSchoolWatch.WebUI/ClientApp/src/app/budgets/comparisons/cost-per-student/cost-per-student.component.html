The amount of money a district spends on each student. Likely the best metric on which to compare the cost-effectiveness of one district versus another. Why so? Unlike tax rates, this number is not influenced by factors such as property values, deficits, increases in property assesments, etc.

Unfortunately, Chichester is far ahead of the pack, spending upwards of 5k+ more per student. Even Chichester's cost-per-student rate 4 years ago exceeds what other Delco districts spend today. (well, except for GV, a much, much wealthier district).

Here's a PSBA publication for the 2017-2018 fiscal year that provides a state-wide view. While other Delaware county districts conform to the state average, Chichester is out in it's own league.

If the community has any interest in putting an end to our excessive taxes, I suggest cost-per-student as the number everyone focuses on. Tax rates (millage) and rate increase %, the preferred metrics of the school board, are far too easily manipulated through smoke and mirror tactics (such as using a deficit to attain a 0% tax rate increase on election year).
